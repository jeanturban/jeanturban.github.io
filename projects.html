The Great Wave

3D Stencil on Acrylic Sheets

This is a stencil I made during my free time this past summer. There were two aspects involved in creating it: the art and the mechanical.

I designed the stencil based on Hokusai's "The Great Wave of Kanagawa" - a rather well know piece of art. Using open source image editors such as GIMP and Inkscape I converted the original piece into having 4 colors only and adjusted the image so that it would be suitable for cutting out. All in all, I am pretty happy with the final look of the stencil in that it is visually pleasing and minimal, all while keeping resemblance to the original piece.

The mechanical aspect involved essentially dealt with making a 3D "canvas" for me to paint on. The idea that led me to paint on acrylic panels was that I could layer them such that it would give the painting more depth. In order to do this I simply cut 4 sheets of acrylic to the appropriate size and cut holes in the corners so that rods could go through them. All of this was done with the laser cutter at my school. Following this, I cut 4 steel rods to the size I wanted (about 4 inches) so that I could line all the panels up and connect them to one another. The last thing I should mention is that I made circular "spacers" out of acrylic to place between panels such that the panels could not wiggle back and forth on the rod.

I painted one layer per panel and then layed all the panels and spacers from the bottom (back) up. I used a small amount of acrylic glue to bond the spacers and panels so that the entire frame was firm. The rods were slotted through the panels and the spacers and ensured that everything was lined up

All in all, I am quite happy with the end result. The colors are bright and pop out really well in lit environments so that's a definite plus. Furthermore, I think my desired effect of depth was achieved and that the stencil turned out great! As a last remark, I think it's worth noting that I experienced minor inaccurices with all the slop from the laser cutter but I was able to get around it by widening the holes in the spacers.
